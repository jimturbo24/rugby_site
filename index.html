Who we are

We are a group of individuals with high level spinal cord injuries (SCI) who get together on a weekly basis to play a game, using special wheelchairs, that allows us to continue to be competitive athletes. Half of our members are former members of the military, while the other half are normal non-service citizens. We all share the same common experiences of living a life with an SCI, which just happens to include a love for the game that we play together. We have been gathering at various locations around San Antonio to play wheelchair rugby for more than 20 years.

our mission

We endeavor to encourage physical activity and provide community for disabled individuals, promote wheelchair rugby as a sport, provide training in the sport of wheelchair rugby, and solicit financial support to promote the health and welfare of team members through the sport of wheelchair rugby.

support us

we are a 503(1)(c) orgainization. Donations are tax deductable. Any monetary support that we recieve is used solely for the following needs:

Equipment

rugby wheelchairs range in price from $4k for a basic frame with no wheels to $8k for a custom frame with two sets of wheels. This is the highest barrier to entry into the sport for newly injured atheletes as these wheelchairs are not covered by any healthcare plans (excluding Veterans benifits).

Travel

given that we are the only wheelchair rugby team in San Antonio and we are members of the USQRA (the national wheelchair rugby league), we must travel to other cities to compete in tournaments with other teams. A single weekend tournament can cost the team $6k for plane flights and lodging for our players.
